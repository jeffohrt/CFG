Canning

Canning uses a water bath to cook (denature proteins, render fat, pasteurize) food in glass jars with special lids that 'snap' down to show they are properly sealed.

Pasteurisation is 70°C for 2 min. This kills all normal bacteria, including botulism but not botulism spores. The botulism toxin is destroyed quickly after 80°C. Killing the spores takes superheated water; 15 PSI (121 °C) for 3 min or 10 PSI (115 °C) for longer. Thus most canning processes are much longer to ensure it is done.

This can't be done in a normal pot at normal pressures. The exception to this is highly acidic foods - like pickles - the acidity kills the spores.

The pressure process can be done in a specialty pot often called a pressure canner.

Soups

Some people pre-cook, slightly more than blanching, the ingredients as an entire soup and then can to complete cooking. Others pack raw, can and must finish cooking when unsealed.

  • Pack jars with soup.

  • Leave normal headspace.

  • Seal and Can normally in a pressure canner.

Meat - lean

Some people brown their meat first in a frying pan to allow them to pour off as much fat as they wish.

  • Remove as much fat as possible.

  • Pack jars with stew meat or ground meat (hamburger).

  • Leave additional headspace.

  • Add water to cover meat and fill to normal headspace.

  • Meat will sink after canning, fat will float.

  • Seal and Can normally in a pressure canner.

Meat - reg

  • Pack jars with stew meat or ground meat (hamburger).

  • Leave additional headspace for the extra fat.

  • Meat will sink after canning, fat will float.

  • Seal and Can normally in a pressure canner.

Meatloaf

  • Mix up your standard meatloaf recipe.

  • Pack the meatloaf into quart jars leaving twice the normal headspace.

  • DON'T add water, the extra fat will fill the additional headspace.

  • Seal and Can normally in a pressure canner.

Baked Beans

I tried raw packing baked beans, after soaking them out. Even at 2/3 beans and 1/3 liquid, the cans were still too dry when finished. 50/50 might work. I'll have to try it sometime.
