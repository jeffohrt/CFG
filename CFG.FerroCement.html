Ferrocement House Project - Part 9 - Cement Sand Fibers Glue
https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7DLe5GJ10CY
  • washed sand - no silt or organic (organic matter will erode concrete in only a few yrs)
  • normal concrete = 1 part portland cement, 2 parts washed sand, 3 parts gravel
  • Type 1 Portland kicks in 30 min, Type 2 kicks in 60
  • Huttash = 2 parts washed sand, 1 part portland cement = mortar or mud
  • fibre reinforced ferrocement - 1 lb fibre per cubic yard cement
  • 1/2 bag cement is 2 inches shy of a 5 gal bucket
  • acrylic concrete glue - a few glugs for each batch as a conditioner, twice that for cold joints
Ferrocement House Project - Part 10 - Retaining wall and Starting the Roof
https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=8Dil0IId5p4
  • Huttash can do 1.5 bags of cement in a good day, his dad averaged 2 when he was that age
  • the fibres make the mud much easier to work with but must be added slowly to mixer
  • fibres prevent cracking in the finished concrete but cause the concrete to kick faster

    → so much faster infact, Huttash, solo, can't use a 1/2 bag mix fast enough ... he and another person could, but solo batches will need a setting retarder

  • the perfect mud will 'slide and flop' in the mixer
  • hose down concrete 2-3 times a day for 2-3 days while it cures slowly for extra strength
