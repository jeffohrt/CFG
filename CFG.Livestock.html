Growing up, my immediate family, my mother, brother and sisters, never had livestock. We had an endless supply of cats I was responsible for. My brother had a rabbit that thought it was a cat. And we had 2 dogs when we were very young.

The family up the road had oxen. The Godfrey's had chickens, hundreds of chickens, that they shared. Rosanne and Larry raised pigs that they shared. I worked for Dennis and Grandpa. They fished and hunted and shared what they got.

I use to visit Blake's house all the time. He had, what seemed at the time, to be every animal on the planet.

I've shot or caught everything. I've never had the chance to raise my own meat animals. I'd like to.

  • 1 cow per acre (leisurely - no rotation) up to 10 cows per acre (intensive, rotational)

  • pasture fees from $0.50 to $2 per head per day or more

Meat Rabbits

  • New Zealand x California cross for meat

  • Breed does at 4 months - younger start = bigger litters

  • Breed bucks at 6 months

  • Retire does at 1-2 yrs - watch litter size and how good of mother they are

  • Bucks breed 3x in minutes, once a day.

  • 1 buck per 5-6 does ... or higher

  • kindle = have a litter of baby bunnies (kits)

  • Handle kits often enough they aren't scared of humans - otherwise butchering is extra unpleasant with screaming, stressed rabbits

  • a rabbit needs 6 ft^2 of grass per day
